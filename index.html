Introduction to New Media


Code and Technology Narrative

This website introduces me and my attitude and usage of technology and digital media. Highlights my attitude toward AI as well as overall technology. It also was my first coding project like this.

Pantone Color of the Year Webtext

This website explores what the Pantone color of the year is. It features several sections of text as well as other new features such as buttons or marquee's.

Going on a Treausre Hunt

This website is a treasure hunt based game that takes you from page to page. Through this webtext I learned how to optimally use buttons to switch between several pages within the same site.

Course Goals


1. Consider technology’s role in acts of making

technology played a huge role in making these webtexts. While coding itself is a form of technology, I had to also use many websites to learn what I was doing. Youtube was a huge resource for me. I watched the video tutorials that were posted to canvas and they were a huge help. I also used the CHAT feature of Visual Code Editor to help me resolve some issues I was having and couldn't figure out myself. I tried my hardest to use my own knowledge and skills to create these webtexts because I wanted to learn but I used technology as a safety net to catch me when I made mistakes. For my Pantone webtext, I had a lot of issues with getting the style right. I spent a large amount of time trying to figure out why my text was not spacing correctly. I asked my peers in workshop and they were confused as well. I spent a lot of time playing around and kept having the same issue. I asked for help from the chat feature and from there I was able to figure out that the margin in style.css was set to the wrong maximum width. Without the use of technology such as ai chat features, I would not have been able to fix the issue I was having.

2. Embrace experimentation

Seeing as this is my first time using code in the way, at first I was really uncomfortable with experimenting. I felt scared that too much experimentation would break the site. As each webtext was created and required more sophistication I felt more and more comfortable playing around with different aspects. For example when I was working on my Pantone webtext, I was able to try out different color combinations and layouts that I wouldn't have thought of otherwise. I experimenting with different aspects within style.css such as background color which made it more visually appealing. In Code and Technology Narrative I felt so out of my comfort zone that they only thing I changed were the places where I needed to add text and the pictures. For the Pantone webtext and the treasure hunt webtext, I was able to experiment with new features like buttons or marquees which felt uncomfortable the first time around. After experimenting though I felt more pride over my webtexts and felt a lot more confidence in coding webtexts to look exactly how I want them to.

3.Engage with others in a workshop environment

Workshop has been very helpful for troubleshooting. I haven't had a lot of issues but when I was having trouble with the spacing and styling of my Pantone webtext, they were able to help me identify that the issue existed in the style.css file. I haven't had many issues with my webtexts but by helping others in workshop as well as seeing what they were doing, I was able to learn from their mistakes and avoid making the same ones. I also learned that there is a lot more potential in these webtexts than I originally thought. My group members go above and beyond on these assignments and create really cool projects. Seeing their creativity as well as success makes me want to work harder to create strong and cool projects. I was having an issue in getting the preview I was seeing in Visual Code Editor to match what I was seeing in the browser. I remembered that one of my group members had the same issue that they fixed by reuploading their files to Github and refreshing the browser. Workshop in this case kept me from turning in a broken webtext.

4. Practice revision within and across media forms

Revision has been a key part of my process in creating these webtexts. I learned that revision is an essential part of creating strong digital media as well as learning and creating stronger and more creative digital media later down the road. I revised my Pantone webtext multiple times to improve its appearance and functionality. Through my revision of the Pantone webtext I learned a lot about what the style.css file contains. Because of my learning about that file I was able to play around with background colors in my other webtexts. For most of my webtexts I realized that I liked the white background more then a different color. For my portfolio however I enjoyed playing around with colors to find one that I liked to show off my learned skill of changing the color. I also revised my treasure hunt webtext because the many buttons caused a lot of issues. I spent a lot of time revising to make sure it ran smoothly and in the end I have a product that I am proud of. I showed it to my friends and they were thoroughly impressed and the smooth mechanics that came from revision helped with that a lot.

5. Learn to present your work

Presenting my work has been a valuable experience in this course. While I have not done it a lot, the times that I did were very helpful. Learning how to effectively explain the work process as well as what my audience is looking at has been a valuable skill to learn. I have also learned a lot about the potential these webtexts have through watching my peers present their work. Moving forward I want to be more confident in presenting my work and explaining the process behind it. The best way to achieve this, is to volunteer myself to present more often. The times that I have presented however have taught me a lot about what exactly it is I need to communicate to show my work without boring everyone. I hope that when I talk about my work I am able to clearly articulate the process and creativity behind it. I want to be able to articulate this too help my peers in the way their presentations have helped me.
